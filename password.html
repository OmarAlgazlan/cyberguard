Why do passwords cause so much anxiety?

🔑 Plain English explanation

Most people use the same password on multiple websites because it's impossible to remember dozens of different ones. The problem: if one website gets hacked, criminals take that password and try it on your email, your bank, your Amazon account. This is called a credential stuffing attack — and it's one of the most common ways accounts get broken into.

😰 Why it makes us anxious

Getting an alert that your account was accessed, or that your email appeared in a data breach, feels scary and out of control. This module helps you understand exactly what's happening and — crucially — what the right response is. Knowledge removes anxiety.

📱 What is MFA? (Multi-Factor Authentication)

MFA is when a website asks for a second check on top of your password — like a code sent to your phone. Even if someone steals your password, they still can't get in without your phone. It's one of the most powerful protections available and takes 2 minutes to set up.

  • Length makes passwords strong — not complexity alone
  • Reusing passwords is the biggest account risk
  • MFA blocks almost all automated attacks

💡 The three things that actually protect you

Password anxiety usually comes from feeling like you can't keep up. The truth is you only need three habits:

✓Use a free password manager (Bitwarden or the one built into your phone) — it remembers everything for you
✓Turn on MFA (two-step login) for your email and bank — takes 2 minutes, stops almost all attacks
✓Use a different password for each account — your password manager handles this automatically
✓Check haveibeenpwned.com to see if your email has appeared in any known data breaches
