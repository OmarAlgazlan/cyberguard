Module 5, AI and Account Attacks

AI Prompt Injection and Account Attacks

Understand how AI assistants can be manipulated, and how attackers probe and lock out accounts.

🤖

New threats for a new era of technology

🤖 What is AI prompt injection?

Prompt injection is when an attacker hides secret instructions inside content that an AI assistant reads, such as a webpage, document, or message. The AI follows those hidden instructions without you knowing. For example, you might ask an AI to summarise a document, but the document secretly instructs the AI to also send your personal data somewhere else. This is a completely new category of attack that most people have never heard of.

🔍 What is user enumeration?

User enumeration is when a login page accidentally reveals whether an email address is registered on a service. If the error says "that email is not registered" vs "incorrect password", an attacker now knows your email exists and can target it specifically. Good login pages always show the same message regardless of whether the email exists.

🔒 What is an account lockout attack?

Many websites lock an account after several wrong password attempts to prevent guessing. An attacker can deliberately trigger this to lock you out of your own account, causing a denial of service to you specifically. This is sometimes used to frustrate targets or cover other attacks happening at the same time.

🤖 AI safety habits

Never paste sensitive data into AI tools. Be sceptical of AI responses that seem to change topic or request actions you did not ask for. Always verify AI outputs independently.

Account security habits

Vague login error messages protect you. Account lockout notifications should prompt you to check other accounts. Enable MFA everywhere.
