What is a phishing email?

📧 Plain English explanation

A phishing email is a fake email designed to trick you. Criminals send emails pretending to be your bank, a delivery company, or a website like PayPal, to get you to click a link or hand over your password. They look very convincing because they copy real logos and language.

😰 Why does it make us anxious?

These emails use panic on purpose, "your account has been suspended", "you owe money", "act within 24 hours". That feeling of urgency is the weapon. When we're scared, we don't think clearly. Recognising that tactic is your best defence.

🎯 Your task in this module

You'll see 5 emails. For each one, decide: is this email real, or is it a scam? Read it carefully, especially the email address it came from, then choose your response. There's no time limit. Take your time.

Inbox, Mail Client

🔍 What to look for

Check the actual email address (after the @ symbol). Does it match the company it claims to be? Scammers use addresses like natwest-alert.com instead of natwest.com.

💡 What to remember

Phishing emails use fear and urgency on purpose. Now that you know the pattern, you can pause and check instead of reacting. That pause is everything.
