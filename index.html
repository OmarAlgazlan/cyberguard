Why cybersecurity anxiety matters

Social engineering attacks deliberately exploit psychological vulnerabilities — urgency, fear, and uncertainty — to provoke impulsive decisions. Many users either freeze or act without thinking when confronted with potential threats.

Traditional cybersecurity training is passive and knowledge-based. CyberGuard takes a different approach: experiential exposure in a safe environment, designed to build genuine confidence alongside awareness.

How it works

01 / ASSESS

Baseline anxiety rating

02 / SIMULATE

Work through realistic threat scenarios as both attacker and defender. Each choice is explained with immediate feedback.

Guidance module

After each scenario, receive targeted anxiety-reduction guidance and confidence-building tips specific to what you just experienced.
