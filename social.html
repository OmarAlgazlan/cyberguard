Module 3, Scam Calls and Messages

Social Engineering

Learn to spot when someone is trying to manipulate you, online, on the phone, or in person.

🎭

What is social engineering?

Social engineering means tricking a person rather than hacking a computer. Attackers manipulate your emotions, such as fear, urgency, and trust, to get you to act without thinking.

😰 Why it makes us anxious

These attacks work by putting you under pressure. "Act now or your account closes." That panic is deliberate. The moment you feel urgency from an unexpected contact, that is your signal to slow down, not speed up.

● 💬 Messages, Mum EXAMPLE: SPOT THE MANIPULATION
Hi love, it's Mum. I've broken my phone and I'm using a friend's number. I'm in a bit of trouble and need you to help me urgently. Can you send £200 to this bank account? I'll pay you back tonight. Please don't call me, I can't talk right now.
Received from: +44 7700 900461 (unknown number)
What do you do?
A. Send the £200 immediately, it could be an emergency
B. Call your mum on her real known number to verify before doing anything
C. Reply asking for more details

🛡️ The one rule that stops almost all social engineering

Always verify through a separate channel. If someone contacts you urgently asking for money or information, hang up or stop messaging and contact them on a number you already know. This one habit protects you from almost every attack.

You will face 5 real scenarios: a scam phone call, a fake job offer, a workplace situation, a "Hi Mum" money scam, and a found USB drive. For each one, identify what is happening and choose the safest response.

💡 This is not about being gullible

Social engineering is designed to bypass your careful thinking by targeting your emotions. Urgency, fear, helpfulness, and love for family, these are normal human traits. Being manipulated doesn't mean you're not smart. It means you're human. The defence is one habit: when something unexpected asks you to act fast, pause and verify independently first.

✓Always verify identity through a separate channel, call back on a number you look up yourself
✓Urgency from an unexpected contact is a red flag, not a reason to act faster
✓It's always okay to say "let me verify this first", anyone legitimate will understand
✓Never plug in a USB drive you found, hand it to IT or security instead
