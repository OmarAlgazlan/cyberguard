Module 4, Unsafe Browsing

Malvertising and Unsafe Browsing

Learn to spot dangerous ads, fake downloads, and deceptive websites before they cause harm.

🌐

Attacks that happen while you simply browse the web

Malvertising means malicious advertising. Criminals use real ad networks to display ads that look normal but redirect you to dangerous sites or trigger malware downloads.

●●● 🌐 https://www.bbc.co.uk/news SPOT THE FAKE AD
You are reading a news article on a legitimate website. An ad appears at the top of the page:
SPONSORED
⚠️ VIRUS DETECTED ON YOUR PC
Windows Defender has found 3 threats. Click to remove them immediately.
Remove Threats Now
✅ Good instinct to question it. This is a classic scareware ad. Windows Defender never communicates through browser ads on news websites. Clicking this button would download malware, not remove it. Real security alerts appear in your system tray, not inside web ads.
Click "Remove Threats Now" to see what happens.

🔒 What is SSL stripping?

When a website shows a padlock icon, the connection is encrypted. SSL stripping silently removes that encryption without you noticing, so everything you type is sent in plain text. Always check the padlock before entering passwords or card numbers.

You will see 4 realistic browsing situations. In each one, identify what is dangerous and choose the safest response.

🌐 Safe browsing habits

Check the padlock before entering any information. Hover over links to see the real destination. Be suspicious of any popup urging immediate action. Use an ad blocker.
