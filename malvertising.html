Module 4, Unsafe Browsing

Malvertising and Unsafe Browsing

Learn to spot dangerous ads, fake downloads, and deceptive websites before they cause harm.

🌐

Attacks that happen while you simply browse the web

📢 What is malvertising?

Malvertising means malicious advertising. Criminals pay real advertising networks to display ads that look completely normal but redirect you to dangerous websites or trigger automatic downloads of malware. You do not have to click anything suspicious, sometimes just loading the page is enough. Even legitimate, trusted websites have served malicious ads without knowing it.

🔗 What is an open redirect?

An open redirect is when a legitimate website's URL is used to secretly forward you somewhere dangerous. The link looks like it goes to a trusted site (like google.com or your bank), but the end of the URL contains hidden instructions that send you somewhere completely different. These are especially dangerous in emails and messages.

🔒 What is SSL stripping?

When a website shows a padlock icon, it means the connection is encrypted and secure. SSL stripping is an attack that silently removes that encryption without you noticing, so everything you type, passwords, card numbers, is sent in plain text that anyone on the same network can read. Always check the padlock before entering sensitive information.

🎯 Your task in this module

You will see 4 realistic browsing situations. In each one, identify what is dangerous and choose the safest response. The attack is shown to you first so you can see exactly how it works, then you decide what to do.

🌐 Safe browsing habits

Check the padlock before entering any information. Hover over links to see the real destination. Be suspicious of any popup urging immediate action. Use an ad blocker.
